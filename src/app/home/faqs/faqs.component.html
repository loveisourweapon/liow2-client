FAQs Frequently asked questions

What is the LIOW campaign?

Love is our Weapon (LIOW) is a bold and creative evangelism initiative that empowers churches, ministries, and groups to impact their communities through intentional acts of love.

Each week, your group focuses on a specific “act of love”, like speaking words of encouragement, being generous, or honouring local heroes. You’ll explore the theme through preaching, small group discussions, and then go out and put it into action!

Your group also gets its own subsite on the LIOW website, where you can track your progress in real-time. As people click “done” and share testimonies, your counter climbs, and your feed fills with stories of transformation.

Who can take part?

Anyone with a heart to see their city transformed by the love of God!Churches, youth and young adult ministries, kids ministries, and community groups, this campaign is for you.

Is there a cost?

Nope, it’s completely free.

How long does the campaign go for?

It’s flexible! You choose how many weeks you want to run it. Most groups find that 5-6 weeks is the sweet spot, but you can go shorter or longer to suit your needs.

Can we choose our own deeds?

Absolutely. You can pick which acts of love to focus on and set the order that works best for your group.

What do we receive when we sign up?

You’ll get access to:

  • Your own group subsite with a live deed tracker
  • A full resource pack including:
    • Social media graphics
    • Printables & handouts
    • Promo videos
    • Weekly deed videos
    • Small group study guides for each deed
    • Youth and kids ministry specific curriculum

Everything you need to make the campaign impactful and easy to run.

Do you provide training?

Yes! A 30-minute Zoom or in-person training session is all it takes to equip your team to launch and lead a successful campaign.

What kind of results have others seen?

Churches and ministries who’ve run the campaign have experienced:

  • Growth in attendance
  • New people coming to services
  • Salvations and recommitments
  • Greater connection and impact in their local communities
